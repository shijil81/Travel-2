Test your endurance on the world's best treks

Inca Trail, Peru

Best hike for modern-day explorers

The 20-mile (33km) trail to Machu Picchu, used for centuries, gained fame when Hiram Bingham 'discovered' it in 1911. Today, it's crowded with backpackers, but the breathtaking views of high cloud forests and the majestic Inca citadel make the journey worth every step.

Distance: 20 miles (33km) round trip

Duration: 4-5 days

Level: moderate

Kilimanjaro, Tanzania

Best trek for: snow in the tropics

The week-long ascent of Africa's highest mountain, Kilimanjaro, is a favorite among fundraisers and East Africa visitors. Its misty peak rises above dusty plains, with lions and elephants at the base. The snow-capped summit, at 19,340 feet (5,895m), poses altitude sickness risks, making it an epic undertaking.

Distance: 11 miles (18km) each way

Duration:2 days

Level: moderate

Everest Base Camp, Nepal

Best trek for: would-be mountaineers

The Everest Base Camp trek is a 2-week adventure reaching 5,545m, offering stunning views of the 8,849m Sagarmatha (Mt Everest). The route winds through river valleys, the Khumbu glacier, mountain monasteries, and Sherpa villages, demanding significant endurance and muscle strength due to challenging altitude gains and gruelling daily hikes.

Distance: 80 miles (130km) round trip

Duration: 2 weeks

Level: moderate
